# Market Sizing — CreatorPulse India

TAM / SAM / SOM for the Indian influencer-marketing opportunity, derived two ways (top-down and bottoms-up) so the figures cross-check. The five-year DCF, NPV, IRR and the take-rate × adoption sensitivity that turn SOM into a valuation live in `CreatorPulse_Executive_Workbook.xlsx`; this document sizes the opportunity those models price.

---

## Headline funnel

| Layer | Definition | Figure (2025) |
|---|---|---|
| **TAM** | Total Indian influencer-marketing spend — the pool CreatorPulse's customers operate in | **₹3,400 cr**, ~25% YoY |
| **SAM** | Mid-market D2C + independent-agency segment realistically addressable by an affordable discovery + screening tool | **₹500 cr** |
| **Tooling-SAM** | Software/tooling spend within SAM (martech/tooling ≈ 3–5% of campaign spend) | **~₹15–25 cr/yr** |
| **SOM** | CreatorPulse achievable annual recurring revenue by year 5 | **~₹6–8 cr ARR** |

---

## TAM — Total Addressable Market

**Top-down.** Independent reports place Indian influencer-marketing spend at **₹3,000–3,600 cr in 2024–2025**, growing ~18–25% YoY: EY projects ~₹3,375 cr by 2026 (~18% CAGR); Kofluence estimates ₹3,000–3,500 cr in 2025 (~22% CAGR); The Goat Agency (WPP Media) / Kantar place 2024 at ₹3,600 cr with +25% in 2025. We anchor **TAM = ₹3,400 cr (2025)** at **~25% YoY** — the midpoint of the mainstream estimates and consistent with the +25% growth multiple reports cite.

**Bottoms-up.** ~12,000–15,000 Indian brands actively run paid influencer campaigns × an average annual influencer budget of ~₹22–28 lakh ≈ **₹3,300–3,600 cr**, reconciling to the top-down anchor.

## SAM — Serviceable Addressable Market

**Top-down.** CreatorPulse v1 targets the **mid-market D2C and independent-agency** segment that is priced out of enterprise tools (~₹2.5L/yr entry) and under-served by directories. That segment represents roughly **15%** of total spend → **~₹500 cr** of campaign spend.

**Bottoms-up.** The serviceable customer base is the mid-market brands (≈ thousands) and independent agencies (≈ hundreds) running multiple briefs a month — Vikram and Sneha in `personas.md`. Their combined influencer-campaign spend reconciles to the ~₹500 cr order of magnitude.

CreatorPulse does not monetise the campaign spend itself; it monetises the **tooling spend** within SAM. At a 3–5% tooling-to-media ratio, the serviceable **tooling-SAM ≈ ₹15–25 cr/yr**.

## SOM — Serviceable Obtainable Market

SOM is CreatorPulse's own achievable ARR, modelled bottoms-up from the freemium-to-paid funnel:

| Driver | Year-5 assumption |
|---|---|
| Paying brand accounts | ~1,200 × ~₹50,000/yr (brand tier, well below the ₹2.5L incumbent entry) |
| Paying agency accounts | ~150 × ~₹1.5L/yr (higher-volume tier) |
| **Year-5 ARR** | **~₹6 cr (brands) + ~₹2.25 cr (agencies) ≈ ₹8 cr** |

That **~₹6–8 cr ARR** represents roughly **30–40% of the serviceable tooling-SAM (₹15–25 cr)** — an aggressive-but-defensible obtainable share for a focused, lower-priced entrant against incumbents that ignore the mid-market and the creator side. The free creator side is treated as customer-acquisition and data cost, not revenue.

## Sensitivity (priced in the Excel DCF)

Year-5 ARR is most sensitive to two levers — **paid-conversion (adoption) rate** and **take-rate / average price per account**. The `Scenarios` sheet of the workbook runs a two-variable data table over adoption × take-rate (base / bull / bear), and the `Financials` sheet carries the five-year revenue/cost/EBITDA build with NPV, IRR and a WACC sensitivity.
